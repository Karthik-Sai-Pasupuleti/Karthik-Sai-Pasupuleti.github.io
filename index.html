Mission

Making Roads Safer

My goal is simple: use intelligent systems to save lives on the road. From developing AI algorithms for driver pose estimation for safe airbag deployment, to building trajectory prediction models for protecting pedestrians and cyclists — every project I work on is driven by this mission.

My journey has taken me from testing L4 autonomous vehicles at Momenta, to engineering collision avoidance systems at SANEON, to building crash prediction deep learning models at VAIVA. Each step has deepened my expertise in computer vision, deep learning, and autonomous driving safety.

Now, as a PhD student at AImotion Bavaria, I'm exploring a new frontier: spiking neural networks — brain-inspired computing that could make autonomous perception dramatically more energy-efficient. The goal is to build an SNN-based perception module with a reasoning layer, deployed on Intel Loihi neuromorphic hardware.

Target Hardware

Intel Loihi 2 — The Target Platform

⬡ Why Neuromorphic Hardware?

Conventional GPUs consume enormous power for autonomous driving perception. Intel's Loihi 2 neuromorphic processor offers a fundamentally different approach — 128 neuromorphic cores with programmable neuron models, on-chip learning, and asynchronous event-driven computation.

My PhD aims to bridge the gap between cutting-edge SNN algorithms and this neuromorphic silicon — deploying real-time perception and reasoning for autonomous vehicles at a fraction of the power budget.
